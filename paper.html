THIS PLAY

Background

Not long ago we considered normal that currency was printed, regulated and controlled by the governments, validated by the corporations. Long before people were introduced to the prejudice that only metal or paper with the faces of famous figures stamped on it constituted real money value was assigned to objects that could be found in the environment. Such objects could be easily used as a liquid medium of exchange accepted by the community, facilitating trade without any outside interference needed. The economy of such communities flourished. Change came as soon as those lands were conquered and brand new coins were minted under the supervision and in full control of the new rulers. As imposed by them, everything else ceased to be legal. Peer-to-Peer electronic cash was introduced almost a decade ago and managed to win a major battle, gaining a new territory of freedom for several years. Even if cryptocurrency had to face enormous amounts of resistance it managed to move forward and gain basic adoption. The backlash came with governments and corporations hard at work finding solutions to limit our new found freedom. It's not hard to notice laws being deployed against privacy and decentralization while in the same time alternatives are being offered as centralized or controlled blockchains. So the solution we are now forced into is the exact problem we were seeking to solve: the fact no central company or government can keep our data secure.

Resolution

Cryptocurrencies can be used as a tool to liberate and protect people, rather than to control them. This will happen through political maneuvering, not technology. We believe we will have the kind of society that most people want. If we want freedom and privacy, we must persuade others that these are worth having. In theory governments represent people, yet when governments are free, the people are enslaved. The true nature of a democratic government is easily represented by a blockchain: formed by the people, asks the people, serves the people. Democracy, in a sense, recognizes the power implicit in large numbers of people. The real nature of a government can be represented as a vulnerable key component in a network. That component can be and is exploited. Once that happens any transfer can be controlled and any malware is free to spread. The way to clean such a network is by exploiting the same weak component. If corrupting the component doesn't require brute force the action can be called lobbying. Lobbying is efficient because it doesn't require vast resources. That means lobbying works with limited resources compared to those available to the component or network. Similar results are achievable by exploiting information leaks, scenario in which potential gain is replaced by potential loss. For our purposes both methods are needed.

Brightness

Cryptocurrencies will create a fifth protocol. This fifth protocol will be able to connect to the existing world and control inputs and outputs independent of the the current financial system. This would finally allow mass adoption and stability. The use of more advanced technology in a full fledged system will support the first financial social network with no owner and no personal information being stored.

Contrast

This is our goal. The notion that we can just fade into crypto and ignore the unpleasant political realities is unrealistic. We are in a race between our ability to build and deploy technology, and their ability to build and deploy laws and restrictions. The possibility of anonymous transactions is vital for an open society. Withdrawing into technology is like pulling the blankets over our heads. It feels good for a while, until reality catches up. We must obtain the security needed to protect our goal, ourselves and our future. Our plan will work because once set in motion: they cannot locate it and they cannot stop it. For privacy to be widespread it must be part of a smart social contract. People must come together and deploy these systems which will serve our common goal. Privacy only extends so far as the cooperation of a movement for the greater good. We will not, however, be moved out of our course because some may disagree with our goals. We cannot expect governments, corporations, or other large, faceless organizations to grant us privacy out of their beneficence.
